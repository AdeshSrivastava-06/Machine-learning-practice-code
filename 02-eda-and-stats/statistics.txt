PROBABILITY RULES 

(a)Addition Rule helps us to find the probability that at least one of 2 events occurs i.e A union B 

P(A U B) = P(A) + P(B)( mutually excluded cannot happen togehter)
P(A U B) = P(A) + P(B) - P(A intersection B)  [can happen togehter]

we subtract to avoid double-counting the overlap
Example-> the probability of drawing a red card or a king (here we have to union)
there are 26 red card and 4 king(2 red and 2 black)
P(A U B) = 26/52 + 4/52 - 2/52

(b)Multiplication is used to find the probability of 2 events happening togehter P(A intersection B)

(c)   Complement Rule
Probability of an event NOT happening:
P(Aᶜ) = 1 – P(A)
Example:
If P(rain) = 0.3 → P(no rain) = 1 – 0.3 = 0.7





CONDITIONAL PROBABILITY
P(A | B) = P(A intersection B) / P(B)
Example:
40% people like tea.
25% like both tea and coffee.

P(Coffee | Tea) = P(Tea ∩ Coffee) / P(Tea)
= 0.25 / 0.40
= 0.625

So, 62.5% of tea lovers also like coffee.
